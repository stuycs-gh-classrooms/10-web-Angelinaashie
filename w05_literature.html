The First Book

The first book is considered to be the ancient "Epic of Gilgamesh," a story about an epic Babylonian king who goes through a series of adventures in the pursuit of immortality. Other very early books from this period include hymns to animalistic deities and other popular stories. These books were generally carved on stone or written on papyrus, not in "codex" form like we have today.

Influencial Literature

Popular works of literature include: Romeo and Juliet, by William Shakespeare, The Republic, by Plato, Metaphysics, by Aristotle, Cantebury Tales by Geoffrey Chaucer, Pride and Prejudice, by Jane Austen, and 1984, by George Orwell. Generally, these books tend to be novels, works of philosophy, or books of poetry or plays. Some non-fiction books have also become quite popular, like The Jungle, by Upton Sinclair, or Down and Out in Paris and London, by George Orwell

Famous Writers

There have been many famous writers, including Leo Tolstoy (War and Peace), Charles Dickens (A Tale of Two Cities, David Copperfield), Lewis Carroll (Alice In Wonderland), Chinua Achebe (Things Fall Apart), and Voltaire (Candide). Famous Writers of the 20th century include Ernest Hemingway and Margaret Atwood.
